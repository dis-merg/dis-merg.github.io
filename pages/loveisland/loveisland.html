The UKs Popularity Contest

Love Island is a popular reality dating show in the UK about male and female contestants living in a villa together trying to find love.

Contestants are added and eliminated throughout the season based on popularity— most often by not being picked by someone of the opposite gender, sometimes by a public vote, and sometimes by a vote within the villa. At the end of the show, four couples go to the finale where the public votes for their favorite, awarding the winners $25k each. Given that this show is essentially a popularity contest, I want to answer the question, “What kind of person is most likely to win Love Island?” I think it will give interesting insight into what people in the UK find desirable.

To answer this question, I compiled a dataset about all of the contestants on the show for seasons 1-8, and then filtered it to only contestants that placed in the top four couples of their season. The variables I'm focusing on in my dataset are:

  • Name
  • Age
  • Gender
  • Race
  • Profession
  • Region of the UK
  • Outcome (winner, runner-up, dumped, etc.)

The characteristics I will be analyzing are age, race, region of the UK, and profession. For each of these aspects, I will look at male and female contestants separately. Given that the show only has cisgendered, straight contestants, there will likely be differences in what makes men vs. women popular.

Young and White

Two fundamental characteristics to consider when figuring out who is likely to win Love Island are age and race. Overall, contestants on the show are primarily in their twenties, with occasional islanders one or two years outside of that in either direction.

Among top female contestants, there is a pretty normal distribution, with the exception of two spikes at ages 20 and 26, and a noticable dip at age 23. Given that the two spikes are quite far from each other with an otherwise normal distribution, it isn't clear from this sample if age has relevance to winning the show, or if age isn't a big factor.

However, just like women, men also have a spike at age 20. The overall distribution of men skews towards the late 20s, so it's surprising that they have a spike at 20 like women do. Because of this, I am going to propose that contestants at age 20 are most likely to do well at the show.

As for race, white contestants are strongly favored for both men and women. Men are more likely to have non-white contestants in the finale, but almost all women that make it that far are white.

Region

Like age and race, region of the UK is similar for both men and women. The East of England region is the most popular for both, followed by the Northwest. One might think that it must mean there are just more people from East of England, but it is actually the third most populous region, after London and Northwest of England.

East of England has some well-known counties like Essex, Cambridgeshire, Norfolk, and Suffolk. Essex is known for having "reality-TV-types," shown in the popular show The Only Way is Essex, which has been running for 29 seasons.

Profession

The profession is where we really start to see differences between men and women. The top jobs for women are model and entertainer, while the top jobs for men are tradesperson and professional athlete. This makes sense, because in society women are often celebrated for their looks, while men are celebrated for physical, "manly" characteritstics.

Conclusion

According to these analyses, the woman most likely to win Love Island would be a 20 year old, white model from East of England. The man most likely to win Love Island would be a 20 year old, white tradesperson from East of England.

Interestingly, neither of these hypothetical people have ever entered the show.

The closest contestants to these characteristics are Katie Salmon, a 20 year old model from Northwest England and Josh Ritchie, a 20 year old tradesperson from Northwest England. I was surprised but excited to see that they both placed in the top four of their seasons (season 1 for Josh and season 2 for Katie)!
